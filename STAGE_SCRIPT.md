## If a judge asks…

**Q: "Why won't Motion just eat you?"**
> "Motion targets sub-100 person teams that already have Slack, Salesforce, and Notion to integrate. Pop targets the 81% of US small businesses that are solo or sub-5. They don't have a tech stack. They have a phone. That's our wedge."

**Q: "Isn't this too broad? Where do you actually start?"**
> "Restaurants in San Francisco. Sub-10 employees. Family-owned. Three letters of intent already verbal. We use the wedge to prove unit economics, then expand laterally to retail, salons, services — anything where the owner is also the operator."

**Q: "Where does GBrain.io specifically help vs. stuffing context in a prompt?"**
> "GBrain holds the operating memory — every email, calendar event, review, supplier interaction across years. A prompt can hold a week. GBrain holds a business. Maria asks 'when did Carlos last raise prices?' — that's a four-year-back lookup. Prompt context can't do that."

**Q: "Hallucination risk — what if Pop sends the wrong invoice?"**
> "Approval gates. Pop drafts everything. Owner taps to send. We learn what they always approve and earn autonomy over time. Same way you trust a new employee."

**Q: "$99/mo — is that high or low?"**
> "It's 1.3 hours of admin time saved per month at the going rate of $20/hr for a bookkeeper. Owners report 16 hours/week on admin. So we need to save them 5% of their admin time to be free."
